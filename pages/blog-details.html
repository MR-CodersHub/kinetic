How Temperature Cycles Affect Windshield Chips & Cracks

Published: May 22, 2026 // By Kinetic Certified Technicians

Modern windshields are made of laminated safety glass: two glass sheets bonded by a clear polyvinyl butyral (PVB) inner layer. When a small stone chips the outer layer, moisture and air enter the pocket. As temperatures fluctuate, the glass expands and contracts. Because glass expands at a different rate than the plastic interlayer, the stress causes micro-fractures to fan outward.

"Even a tiny chip can turn into a spiderweb crack overnight when the morning sun hits a cold windshield."

To prevent a chip from turning into a costly crack, technicians inject a specialized, clear optical resin. This resin bonds with the inner glass layer under vacuum, restoring structural strength and optical clarity. Getting chips repaired early saves you from needing a complete windshield replacement.
