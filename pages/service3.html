// Calibration Inquiries

Q// What is the difference between Static and Dynamic Calibration?

Static calibration takes place in a controlled shop setting using targeted measuring boards. Dynamic calibration requires driving the vehicle on the road at specific speeds under clear conditions to allow the camera system to calibrate against road lines.

Q// Can your mobile units perform ADAS calibration at my home?

Yes. Our mobile response units are equipped with portable target boards, laser alignment rigs, and diagnostic computers, allowing us to perform both static and dynamic calibrations on site.

// SERVICE TIMELINE

The Calibration Process Steps

01 // MEASUREMENT

Laser Target Setup

Aligning physical target boards relative to the vehicle's thrust direction using advanced laser measures.

02 // CONNECTION

ECU Diagnostics

Connecting a diagnostic tablet to the vehicle's OBD-II port to initialize camera calibration mode.

03 // ALIGNMENT

Camera Calibration

Adjusting safety camera offsets via calibration tools and factory-approved procedures to re-center field calculations back to original factory presets.

04 // VALIDATION

Certificate Lock

Performing a diagnostic system scan and printing a certified safety report ensuring systems are operational.
