Why You Need ADAS Calibration After Windshield Replacement

Published: April 14, 2026 // By Kinetic Certified Technicians

Modern vehicles are equipped with Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS). These systems rely on forward-facing cameras and sensors mounted directly on the inside of the windshield. When a windshield is replaced, the camera is unmounted and then reattached to the new glass. Even a microscopic difference in the camera's angle or positioning can result in significant errors down the road.

"An offset of just one degree on a windshield-mounted camera can cause the system to miscalculate lane boundaries or stopping distances by several meters at highway speeds."

To ensure your safety, auto glass industry standards require a complete recalibration of these cameras post-replacement. Our certified technicians perform both static and dynamic calibrations to align the sensors precisely with your vehicle's physical steering axis. This ensures features like Lane Keep Assist (LKA) and Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) function exactly as the manufacturer intended.
