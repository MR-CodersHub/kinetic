OEM vs. Aftermarket Auto Glass: Curvature & Strength Comparison

Published: March 9, 2026 // By Kinetic Certified Technicians

When booking a windshield replacement, customers are often asked to choose between Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) glass and aftermarket alternatives. OEM glass is produced by the same manufacturer that supplied the original factory glass for your vehicle, matching its exact thickness, light transmission, curvature, and acoustic ratings.

"Aftermarket glass is designed to fit your vehicle's window frame, but minor discrepancies in thickness or sensor bracket placement can impact HUD display clarity or ADAS cameras."

While aftermarket glass is typically more affordable and meets all DOT safety regulations, OEM glass ensures a perfect match for vehicles with advanced built-in features, such as rain sensors, acoustic insulation, or head-up displays (HUDs). At Kinetic, we guide you through this choice to find the best option for your vehicle and budget.
